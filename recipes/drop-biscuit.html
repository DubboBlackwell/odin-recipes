Air-Fryer Drop Biscuits

Southern inspired simple air fryed biscuits

This recipe puts your air-fryer to good use.

Inspired from the American South, these biscuits use common pantry ingredients, to create a quick fix for your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sifted Bread or AP Flour (makes 4 biscuits)
  • 1 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (for browning)
  • 80g extremely cold Unsalted Butter
  • 3/4 cup Buttermilk or any Milk
  • *2 tablespoons lemon juice if using Milk*

Step-by-Step

Prep work

  1. Mix dry ingredients together (Flour, Baking Powder, Salt, Sugar) Optionally place in freezer
  2. Use a large cheese grater to grate the butter or chop butter into dime-sized pieces
  3. Mix butter with dry ingredient and gently coat and squish the butter until all are covered with flour
  4. Add Buttermilk or Milk/Lemon mixture (see instructions below) with the Flour Butter mixture
    • *Add 2 tablespoons of Lemon Juice to Milk and set aside for 5 minutes*
  5. Gently mix being careful not to overwork the dough
  6. Dump mixture to working surface and gently flatten it out to about 1/2-3/4 inch thickness
  7. Fold one side on top of the other, flatten and repeat 4-5 times
    • *There will be plenty loose flour in the beginning, just add the loose flour on top before folding*
  8. After last fold, only flatten dough to about 1-1.5 inches thick
  9. Use a cookie cutter (or cut) the dough in your preferred shape
  10. Place dough into a preheated Air-Fryer and cook at 160 for 8-12 minutes
  11. Serve with or gravy like a real Southerner!
